Kepler-452b

              From about 30 years ago, there was not any scientific proof of a planet outside our solar system. But thanks to advanced technology like (HWO) we have discovered thousands of exoplanets, which made humans ask themselves (Are we alone in the universe?) Designing a habitable world involves considering various factors that support life as we know it. These include the mass, the radius, gravity, the planet's distance from its host star (it should be in the habitable zone), atmosphere, magnetic field, among others.

              Firstly, we have used Proxima Centauri b because of its suitable mass and radius which are 1.07 times the mass of the Earths, and 1.03 times the radius of the Earth. This combination will lead to a gravity like the Earth of about 1.008 times the Earth (by the law of gravitational field which is equal to GM/r2). It also will give us the same mass of the atmosphere like the Earth.

              Secondly, the sun of our planet will be a G-type star called Kepler-452 b, which is approximately like our sun, only 3.7% more massive and 11% larger. Its temperature is nearly like our sun which is 5757 K. Our planet will rotate in the orbit of Kepler-452b which will lead to a temperature of 265 K (−8 °C; 17 °F).

              Thirdly, determination of climate and temperature have an important impact. The equilibrium temperature recorded on Kepler 452 b suggests the possibility of a climate like that of Earth, perhaps allowing for the presence of liquid water on its surface. This trait is crucial in nurturing and sustaining life as we know it.

              Fourthly, we determined the composition of the atmosphere, which is composed of nitrogen, oxygen, other gases like carbon dioxide. We choose these components because; nitrogen helps in maintaining atmospheric pressure and is an essential nutrient for many organisms, oxygen allows organisms to convert food to energy, and carbon dioxide helps regulate the planet's temperature by trapping heat. The planet must contain a soil to produce nitrogen from bacteria, also there must be plants to provide us by oxygen through photosynthesis. This cycle will keep the percentage of components of atmosphere the same over time.

              Fifthly, magnetic field is produced from conducting electricity because the core contains conductivity material which is iron or nickel and this result a magnetic field in our planet. The importance of magnetic field is protection atmosphere from harmful radiation by deflecting charged particles.

              To conclude that, the past 30 years have brought remarkable advancements in our understanding of exoplanets, fueling our curiosity about life beyond our solar system sun. By considering factors like mass, radius, gravity, distance from the star, atmosphere, and magnetic field, we can envision habitable worlds with conditions similar to Earth. These discoveries pave the way for further exploration and the possibility of finding life elsewhere in the vast expanse of the universe.
